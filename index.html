Millennials: Travel Habits and Where They're Going

by Joanna Williams

A study conducted by Eventbrite concluded that 78% of American millennials would rather spend money on meaningful experiences or live events as opposed to tangible items. To millennials, many of these desired “meaningful experiences” include traveling.

According to a 2018 vacation survey from Travelport in which 1,500 individuals in the United States were asked about their vacation spending, one third of millennials said they would be willing to spend over $5,000 on vacations for the year. Millennials also planned to increase their travel more than individuals in older generations. Travelport found that of the individuals surveyed, 56% of millennials hoped to travel more in the summer of 2018 than they had the previous summer, while only 35% of Gen X and 22% of Baby Boomers said they planned to travel more in 2018.

In a 2016 survey conducted by Airbnb that surveyed millennials in the US, UK, and China, majority of the individuals surveyed indicated they value traveling more than buying a house. In particular, Chinese Millennials prioritized traveling as more important than paying off debt or purchasing a home or car.

Millennials’ travels are also more heavily influenced by social media than previous generations.

According to research from the Internet Marketing Inc., 87% of millennials use Facebook for travel inspiration, while 97% of millennials post their travel experiences on social media, and 75% post to social media every day.

In contrast, a Jetsetter survey concluded that when combining multiple generations, only 60% of travelers post their trips to social media.

Eventbrite credits some of millennial travelers’ social media habits to the idea of “FOMO” or “fear of missing out,” stating that millennials are motivated to post their trips in order to blend in with peers and “not miss out.”


So where are all these millennials actually traveling or wanting to travel to?

Earth, a travel media company based in New York City, used data from over 100,000 of their millennial Instagram followers from July 2017 to 2018 to rank the top 10 travel destinations amongst millennials.


Earth ranked the following places as the top millennial travel destinations:

1.Tulum, Mexico
2. Amsterdam, Netherlands
3. Greek Islands (Santorini, Mykonos, Corfu, and Naxos)
4. Mexico City, Mexico
5. The Maldives
6. Algarve, Portugal
7. Marrakech, Morocco
8. Positano, Italy
9. Cappadocia, Turkey
10. Bali, Indonesia
